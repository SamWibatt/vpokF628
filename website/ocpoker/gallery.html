Readers' Gallery

Some readers have sent me pictures of their versions of One Chip Video Poker. They're shown here with the newest ones at the top.

If you build it and want me to put a picture of your version up here, please let me know.

Click the thumbnails for larger views of the images.

Erik Jenkins returns with an outstanding implementation housed in an oak box with a polycarbonate face. Terrific work, Erik!

JohnC's version housed nice and snug in a small plastic/aluminum project box. The red switch on the back controls the LCD backlight. He modified the game's payouts a bit and reports that “the grandkids love it.”

That's what I like to hear!

Wow - two reader emails in one day!

Here is Dmitrij Kazakov's very professional - looking version. The case is a repurposed old D-SUB splitter housing with the game's buttons on the back.

Thank you, Dmitrij!

Breadboarded version by Ray, who reports that he found the game is good and bug-free. Thanks, Ray!

Erik Jenkins' superb 16F628A version, housed in a tin and featuring both an inverse backlit LCD and a nifty retro power switch.

Breadboarded prototype by Eddy Nijland. Eddy reports that using a 16F628A worked fine.

John Williams' version, handsomely built on an experimenter board. John also used a 16F628A.

He also sends a thumbs-up review - “My girlfriend loves to play it.” I'm always glad to know people get a kick out of it.
